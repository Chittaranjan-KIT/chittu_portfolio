CFD-Based Thermal Management of an Electric Truck Battery Cooling System

3-phase end-to-end simulation: drive cycle analysis, 1D battery thermal model, and 2D CHT cold plate simulation for the Mercedes-Benz eActros 600.

+
Phase 1 - Drive Cycle Analysis (Python)

Extracted the VECTO Long Haul cycle (92,937 data points at 1 Hz) of a 40-ton truck of an electric variety. Calculated battery heat generation Q(t) dynamically during a 2-hour highway drive by using the vehicle dynamics formulae for drag, rolling resistance, grade resistance, and inertia. Physical clipping of the maximum power to 600 kW was carried out according to the specs of the eActros 600.

Phase 2 - 1D Battery Thermal Model (MATLAB)

Developed a lumped capacitance-based battery thermal model with a closed-loop coolant system (UA=2000 W/K cold plate and ethylene glycol water coolant). Performed a parametric study on ambient temperature variations (0 °C winter/23 °C nominal/40 °C summer). Concluding result - passive cooling is ineffective above 40 °C ambient temperature; an active chiller becomes essential during summers, with battery temperature reaching up to 46 °C.

Phase 3 - 2D CHT Cold Plate Simulation (MATLAB)

Designed a steady-state 2D Conjugate Heat Transfer simulation based on finite differences method and Dittus-Boelter Nu equation for k-omega SST turbulence proxy. The boundary conditions used were those from Phase 2 (worst case scenario) where season is summer at 40°C, heat flux = 2076 W/m², velocity = 0.5 m/s, and inlet temperature = 298 K. Results include: maximum wall temperature = 26.1°C (comfortably below 60°C design specification), pressure drop = 255 Pa (not a significant pump loss), convection HTC = 2037 W/m²K, Re = 2351 transitional regime.

Virtual Validation of Wheel-Fastener Preload & Self-Loosening

End-to-end simulation of the fastener holding a high-performance wheel to its hub: clamp-force & friction modelling, preload-scatter analysis, and transverse-vibration self-loosening (Junker mechanism).

+
Description

A complete virtual-validation study built around the single quantity that governs joint safety - the clamp force (preload). The work first models how tightening torque converts into clamp force using the VDI 2230 torque–tension framework, decomposing the torque into its thread-friction, under-head-friction, and bolt-stretch components, then runs a Monte-Carlo study to quantify how real friction variation propagates into preload uncertainty. It then models how that preload is lost in service: a reduced-order (1-DOF) simulation of the Junker self-loosening mechanism, where transverse vibration shears the joint and unwinds the nut. Deliverables include preload-scatter plots, clamp-force decay curves, a loosening-threshold map, and a real-time animation of the joint losing its grip.

Key Results
  • Nominal preload 71.6 kN on an M14 wheel bolt at 160 N·m (bolt stress 575 MPa); nut factor K = 0.16, validated against the published lubricated-steel range
  • Torque decomposition: only ~11% of applied torque stretches the bolt - ~40% lost to thread friction, ~49% under the bolt head
  • Friction variation alone drives ~11% scatter in achieved preload (60.5–86.4 kN, 5th–95th percentile); doubling thread friction collapses preload by −29%
  • Self-loosening shows a sharp threshold at 0.10 mm transverse amplitude - below it the joint holds indefinitely; above it preload decays exponentially
  • Quantified loosening rate: 0.18 mm amplitude → 50% preload loss in ~347 cycles; 0.35 mm → ~111 cycles, reproducing real Junker-rig behaviour (DIN 25201 / ISO 16130)

GenAI Design Co-Pilot - Aero-Thermal Vehicle Concepts

A generative-AI pipeline that turns a plain-English design brief into optimized, physics-validated vehicle concepts: generate, surrogate-evaluate, optimize, validate.

+
Description

An end-to-end generative design system for early-stage vehicle concepts. A conditional VAE generates parametric geometry from target specs, ML surrogate models predict aerodynamic drag and peak battery temperature in milliseconds (replacing expensive CFD/Simulink inside the optimization loop), and a constraint-aware optimizer drives the design into the feasible, low-drag region. Top concepts are then verified against independent physics. Unlike a single-physics demo, it couples aerodynamics and EV thermal management, the exact systems-engineering capability OEMs and Tier-1 suppliers are investing in.

Key Highlights
  • Two coupled physics domains - aerodynamics (Cd) + EV thermal (peak battery temp), with cross-domain coupling: aero shape affects cooling airflow affects battery temperature
  • AI surrogates replace simulation in the loop - Gradient Boosting models score ~110,000 designs/second; aero R² = 0.99, thermal R² = 0.97
  • Generative optimization - cooling feasibility lifted from a 26% random baseline to ~98% under targeted conditional generation
  • Physics-validated - top designs verified against independent physics: surrogate Cd error 0.39%, thermal error ~2.8 °C; all feasible
  • Natural-language interface - type a design brief and get a live CFD-style concept visualization across six archetypes (City, Sport, Sedan, GT, SUV, Track)
